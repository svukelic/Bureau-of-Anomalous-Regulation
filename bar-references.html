The following hazard designations document the primary risk vectors associated with each anomaly. Personnel must be certified in all applicable hazard categories prior to engagement with the corresponding anomalous entity.

Protocol Regulations

The following protocols define operational standards for handling anomalies based on their primary hazard characteristics. Protocol assignment is determined by hazard classification and weighting.

White Horse Protocol

White Horse Protocol addresses anomalies that primarily interact with information, perception, and consciousness. These anomalies typically manipulate how reality is perceived rather than reality itself, affecting cognitive processes, sensory input interpretation, or awareness expansion.

Key operational considerations include information compartmentalization, observer rotation, cognitive monitoring, and memetic security measures.

Red Horse Protocol

Red Horse Protocol addresses anomalies that manipulate, transform, or emit energy or matter in ways that violate standard physics but maintain some consistency. These anomalies have observable physical effects, interact with conventional matter, and can be detected through instrumentation.

Key operational considerations include physical containment architecture, energy management, emission shielding, and environmental protection measures.

Black Horse Protocol

Black Horse Protocol addresses anomalies that demonstrate agency or independent decision-making. These anomalies may actively resist or circumvent containment, adapt to containment measures, manipulate personnel or systems, or utilize deception or mimicry.

Key operational considerations include psychological containment strategies, adaptive security measures, deception countermeasures, and behavior pattern analysis.

Pale Horse Protocol

Pale Horse Protocol addresses anomalies that alter fundamental aspects of reality. These anomalies may violate basic physical laws, create inconsistent or unpredictable effects, affect the structure of space, time, or causality, or fundamentally rewrite the rules of existence.

Key operational considerations include reality anchoring technology, causality monitoring, exotic containment methods, and contingency planning for unpredictable effects.
