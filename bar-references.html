Bureau of Anomalous Regulation

Classification Reference

Behavior Classification

The following designations categorize anomalies according to their fundamental operational mechanisms and their documented effects on objective reality. This classification determines primary containment protocols and research methodologies.
ClassEffect on Reality
White HorseExhibits properties that modify perceptual frameworks and information accessibility. Facilitates unauthorized sensory input and cognitive expansion beyond standard parameters.
Red HorseGenerates destabilization in established systems through introduction of emotional, physical, or logical irregularities. May precipitate cascading failure in dependent structures.
Black HorseManifests manipulation of standard entropic processes. Demonstrates capacity to redistribute, condense, or reorganize fundamental physical or metaphysical constants.
Pale HorseDemonstrates capacity to nullify, invert, or fundamentally alter baseline reality constructs including but not limited to: consciousness, causality, object permanence, and identity cohesion.

Hazard Categories

The following hazard designations document the primary risk vectors associated with each anomaly. Personnel must be certified in all applicable hazard categories prior to engagement with the corresponding anomalous entity.
CodeLabelMeaning
AAutonomousEntity demonstrates independent decision-making processes and/or exhibits non-standard behavioral patterns without external catalysts. Requires algorithmic prediction models to anticipate response variables.
BBiologicalPossesses capacity to replicate, propagate, or modify organic material through direct or indirect exposure. May induce cellular restructuring or genetic recoding in standard biological systems.
CCognitiveDemonstrates capability to restructure, impair, or otherwise manipulate standard neurological processes including but not limited to: memory formation, perceptual input processing, and logical sequencing.
DDimensionalExhibits properties that contravene standard Euclidean parameters. May create topological inconsistencies or generate access points to non-standard spatial domains.
EElectromagneticGenerates, manipulates, or otherwise affects electromagnetic spectrum phenomena outside conventional physical parameters. May interfere with standard measurement and communication systems.
FFunctionalMaterial object exhibiting consistent, replicable anomalous properties. Operational methodology may be documented but remains inconsistent with known physical principles.
HHostileDemonstrates active intention to compromise Bureau personnel or infrastructure. May employ direct physical means or indirect methods to undermine containment protocols.
IImitativePossesses capacity to assume properties, appearance, or behavioral patterns of other entities. May compromise identification and authentication protocols.
MMetaphysicalOperates through conceptual frameworks typically resistant to standard measurement methodologies. May require symbolic or abstracted containment procedures to maintain stability.
PPerceptualInduces non-standard sensory input or processing in subjects. Effects may persist after exposure termination and remain resistant to cognitive recalibration procedures.
TTemporalDemonstrates ability to manipulate conventional causality chains or linear time progression. May generate localized chronological inconsistencies or paradoxical event sequences.
UUndefinedProperties and operational mechanics remain incompletely documented or understood. Research protocols in active development. All engagement requires executive authorization.
